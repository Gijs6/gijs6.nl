---
title: Dear boomers
---

Dear boomers, elders, fossils, and other 20th-century people,

I have some bad news: it is not "the past" anymore. I know that can be uncomfortable, especially since many of you remember the past as something better, and use it as a reference point where everything today looks worse by comparison.

But there is worse news: it will not become the past again. Not because anyone is refusing to go back, but because the past isn't a place we can return to. It was a specific combination of people, knowledge, technology, economics, and social norms that no longer exists. The world changed. Quite a lot, actually.

What used to be possible is not automatically a valid benchmark for what should be expected now.

You often use arguments like "you could just do X back then" or "that wasn't a problem before" as criticism of younger generations. But those aren't really arguments about the present. They're comparisons with a different world, under different conditions, with different rules.

Saying "I bought a house at 24" is not economic analysis. Saying "I didn't need a phone all day at school" is not an argument that phones are unnecessary for students (in fact, they are quite necessary every single day for every single student, thanks to the systems **you** and **your generation** set up). These are personal anecdotes from a world that no longer exists.

**Having lived through earlier versions of society gives perspective, but it does not make that perspective applicable today.** The fact that you were young once does not make you an expert on today's youth any more than owning a steam train makes you an expert on modern urban rail systems.

You are absolutely allowed to dislike the changes, criticise them, or even try to fix what went wrong. But it only works if you start from the recognition that *everything* has changed.

Kind regards,  
The present
