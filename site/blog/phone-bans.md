---
title: On school phone bans
---

Pretty much all Dutch schools already have a phone ban or are considering one. And I think this is unfair.

Let me first say, I completely agree that phones do not belong in classrooms during lessons. They are distracting and addictive, and they take away your focus during the lesson. Banning phones during lessons is completely fair and understandable.

But what most Dutch schools are doing is an outright ban on phones in the entire school building, which is indefensible in my opinion. Why would you not be allowed to have your phone in the hallway or during breaks? It's not like you're learning less just because you're looking at a screen instead of the floor while walking through school.

And what exactly is the point of banning phones during <i lang="nl">tussenuren</i>? What goal are you even trying to achieve? There's no lesson being disrupted, and nobody is losing focus. Students just have a free period and nothing to do, and Dutch schools can have A LOT of <i lang="nl">tussenuren</i>. You're just taking something away for no reason whatsoever.

Oh and if we're doing this, it should apply to everyone equally. Teachers, support staff, the janitor, the secretary, everyone. You cannot tell students they can't have their phones while teachers and staff walk around with theirs all day. And I can already hear the teachers say, "Oh but what if there is an emergency and I need to be called?" LIKE STUDENTS DON'T HAVE EMERGENCIES WHEN THEY SHOULD BE CALLED? "Oh yes no but it is essential for me to access the attendance system." WELL IT IS ALSO ESSENTIAL FOR US TO CHECK OUR SCHEDULE WHICH IS IN THE SAME DAMN SYSTEM.

Also, education has a big role in preparing children for society, and I hate to break it to you, but everyone in society uses mobile phones all the time. So it is way better to teach the children how to use their phones wisely and warn them about the dangers instead of just banning phones altogether. And spoiler: in higher education, phones are just allowed too. So you're not preparing us for society, and you're not even preparing us for the next step in our education. Great job.

On top of that, you cannot make all of our education systems digital and then take away our phones. I am absolutely outraged when I hear an adult (most often teachers) being angry at the fact that students are "all looking at their phones when walking between lessons" WHEN THOSE VERY TEACHERS ARE THE ONES THAT SET UP SYSTEMS WHERE THE ONLY PLACE WE CAN SEE OUR SCHEDULE IS OUR PHONE. YOU CANNOT MAKE US DEPEND ON OUR PHONES FOR SCHOOL AND THEN GET ANGRY WHEN WE USE THEM.

Completely banning phones in the entire school is total overkill, and it can hurt people too. There are quite a few people without any friends or anyone to talk to during breaks, who use their phones to distract themselves by watching a movie or listening to music. Oh, I don't know if you have realised this yet, but this also means that they're practically banning listening to music in school, which is completely ridiculous.

And if you're genuinely worried about phone use, there are so many better things you could do. Add more activities during breaks and <i lang="nl">tussenuren</i>. Give students something to actually do. Make the school day more engaging. Put that energy into actually fixing things instead of just taking something away.

Oh and maybe, if students are spending entire lessons on their phones instead of paying attention, maybe the lesson just isn't interesting enough. Maybe the education itself is the problem.

The moment a school starts banning phones in hallways and during <i lang="nl">tussenuren</i>, they've stopped caring about education and started caring about creating an authoritarian, control-obsessed environment. And we're just supposed to accept that.
